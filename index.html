Combined maximum exceeded — LAST risk

Stop injecting. If systemic toxicity develops: call for help and the LAST rescue kit, give 20% lipid emulsion 1.5 mL/kg bolus (then 0.25 mL/kg/min), manage airway with 100% O₂, treat seizures with benzodiazepines, and avoid local anesthetics, β-blockers & calcium channel blockers during ACLS; reduce epinephrine boluses to ≤1 µg/kg. Consider ECMO early.

ASRA anticoagulation guide

Hold and restart intervals for neuraxial and deep plexus/peripheral blocks, per the ASRA Pain Medicine evidence-based guidelines, 5th edition (Jan 2025).
No agents match that search.
Scope notes. Deep plexus and deep peripheral blocks follow the same rules as neuraxial techniques; for superficial/compressible sites, weigh compressibility, vascularity, and the consequence of bleeding. Recommendations assume monotherapy — combinations of hemostasis-altering drugs are additive. In select high-risk parturients needing urgent intervention, general anesthesia may carry more risk than neuraxial, and exceptions may be appropriate.

Source: Kopp SL, Vandermeulen E, McBane RD, et al. Regional anesthesia in the patient receiving antithrombotic or thrombolytic therapy, ASRA Pain Medicine Evidence-Based Guidelines (5th ed). Reg Anesth Pain Med, Jan 2025.
